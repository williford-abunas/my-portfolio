My Wellbeing Plan according to Te Whare Tapa Wha Model

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

The 4 dimensions are:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whanau (family wellbeing)

With 4 walls, the wharenui is a symbol of these 4 dimensions. The wharenui has connections to the whenua (land, sea, natural world) which forms the foundations for the four dimensions


My Personal Plan

Taha Whanau (Family wellbeing)

  • Call family at least a fortnight
  • Hug my wife everyday
  • Daily chat with flatmates
  • Give compliments at least once a day
  • Spend time with wife doing leisure activities a week

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

  • Engage in physical activities at least 30 mins. each day
  • Eat veggies and fruits every other day or as often
  • Get 6 to 8 hours of sleep everyday
  • Drink plenty of water

Taha Wairua (Spiritual wellbeing)

  • Engage in meditation at least 5 mins. everyday
  • Go to church every Sunday
  • Start and end the day with a prayer

Taha Hinengaro (Mental wellbeing)

  • Practice breathing exercises when stressed or feeling overwhelmed
  • Think of positive things that happened within the day
  • Apply growth mindset, i.e., think of failures as challenges
  • Being kind to one's self

Whenua (Connection to the land)

  • Engage in daily walks around neighborhood
  • Spend 5 minutes listening to nature sounds a day
