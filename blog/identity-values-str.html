Identity, Values and Strengths

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

In my previous job as a teacher, there was an opportunity for promotion that I applied for. There were multiple candidates who vied for the slots by racking up points in different areas like professional development participations and further studies. I had my recent achievements appraised with confidence to be among the top candidates. Since I am aware of how the point system goes, I had a rough estimate of my score. Before the results were published I had the opportunity to peek at it and I was surprised that I got a score higher than I expected. When I analyzed the breakdown, I realized that the secretary accidentally added twice the amount of points in one of the areas. If I remember correctly, I was about 2 points ahead of the third in rank which gives me a big chance to have that promotion. But my conscience did not succumb to the temptation. Even though that promotion could make a huge difference, I told the secretary with honesty about the error making the candidate who is behind me take my place in the ranking and eventually got the promotion. Sometimes I think that if I just kept quiet I would have been promoted but I never regretted my decision because it was the right thing to do besides my conscience is free.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My family taught me to be kind, caring and generous. I grew up relying on my relatives' help and they are very much willing to extend that help to anyone in need without asking for something in return. I have a religious upbringing and I was exposed to church activities at a young age which prompted me to enter the seminary. There, I studied Philosophy where it was supposed to help me broaden my understanding about spirituality but instead grappled me with questions which I still haven’t figured out. So, I went out of the seminary, though I can still see myself as a devout Catholic with an on and off relationship with the Church. Nevertheless, I have learned a lot as a seminarian as it was my training ground which honed me in areas of leadership, communication, collaboration and autonomy. After that, I met my soon to be girlfriend and now my wife who oriented me to the workings of the emotions. Before, I was a bit intolerant with things outside of reason but in our rollercoaster ride of a long distance relationship it developed in me a sense of purpose that I do not exist for myself but for others especially to those who are close to my heart.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

According to the dope test, I am an OWL (45%) which means I am a detail seeker and have low assertiveness and emotionality. Also, I tend to be more logical, mathematically minded, methodical and sometimes seen as perfectionist. I can be slow to make decisions and inflexible if rules and logic says otherwise. I am not a big risk taker but love detail. I certainly agree with the results, especially with my personality and strengths. I am a logical and methodical thinker who often would question things that are unreasonable and seem out of order. This is simply demonstrated when shopping. I often wonder if the item is necessary like "why buy shoes when I still have a couple" or "it's a good deal but we don't have our own house yet". Also, I like to do everything in order and follow each step sequentially. This can be a strength to manage impulses but a weakness when making quick decisions.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have no coding or programming experience and I still struggle to find my routine. I can’t do anything with the first one but it shouldn’t be a worry as the course is tailored for beginners like me but the second one can hugely impact my learning journey. Moreover, it is important to find the right balance of pushing oneself and allowing oneself to rest when considering wellbeing. My goal at the end of foundations but hopefully before that is I establish a routine for myself that takes wellbeing into account and recognize that there are support systems available.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I recognize that working with others is one of my weaknesses not because I am too overbearing but because I tend to keep to myself. I used to have a really low self-esteem that whenever there was a group discussion I used to think my ideas were less important. A lot of these instances happened in my previous job as a teacher in a new country. Although the team is very supportive and understanding, I was not proactive enough to contribute. At times, I tried to suck it up and express myself but I reverted to being a wallflower when my ideas were not recognized. I could change this by listening more and asking questions in a timely manner when I can’t understand something because basically, collaboration won’t happen when there is no effective communication.
